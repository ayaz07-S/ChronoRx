Fix Your Sleep. Play Better. Feel Less Burnt Out.
ChronoRx for Teens — your body clock, explained simply
Why This Matters for Your Age Group
You know that feeling when you can't sleep until 2am no matter how hard you try? That's not laziness — that's biology. Teen brains naturally shift to a later sleep phase. ChronoRx understands your body clock and helps you work with it, not against it.
Circadian disruption during adolescence is linked to lower academic performance, mood instability, increased anxiety, and higher risk of depression. Getting your sleep timing right isn't just about feeling good — it directly affects your grades, your sport performance, and your mental health.
Your Biggest Circadian Challenges
Social jetlag: Your school starts at 8am but your body wants to sleep until 10am. ChronoRx calculates your ideal schedule and helps you shift gradually.
Screen light at night: Blue light from your phone delays melatonin by up to 3 hours. ChronoRx shows you the exact minute to dim your screen.
Inconsistent weekends: Sleeping in on weekends resets your body clock every Monday. The app detects this pattern and shows you the cost.
ADHD medication timing: Stimulants taken too late disrupt sleep. ChronoRx shows the optimal window to avoid this.

ChronoRx for Young Adults — performance, productivity, and medication timed to your biology
Why This Matters for Your Age Group
You have 4–6 hours of peak cognitive performance per day. Most people spend them in meetings, commuting, or scrolling. ChronoRx maps your exact peak window and helps you schedule everything — work, workouts, and medication — inside it.
Young adults are the most circadian-disrupted demographic globally. Shift work, irregular sleep, late-night screens, and high-stress schedules cause chronic social jetlag that accumulates silently. Research links this to metabolic disorders, burnout, and reduced medication efficacy for conditions like anxiety, ADHD, and thyroid disorders.
Your Biggest Circadian Challenges
Social jetlag: Working 9–5 when your biology peaks at noon–8pm. The mismatch accumulates like a debt.
ADHD & anxiety meds: Stimulants and SSRIs have specific circadian windows for optimal absorption. Most prescriptions ignore this entirely.
Contraceptive pill timing: Effectiveness and side effects vary with circadian phase. ChronoRx gives the evidence-backed optimal window.
Gym timing: Muscle performance peaks at 4–6pm for most chronotypes. Training at 7am is suboptimal by design.

🏠  Adults & Parents
Age Range: 36–59 years
You're Managing Multiple Medications. Make Sure Timing Isn't Working Against You.
ChronoRx for Adults — polypharmacy management, caregiver coordination, and clinical-grade circadian scheduling
Why This Matters for Your Age Group
By your 40s, the average person manages 2–4 medications across different conditions, often prescribed by different doctors who never talk to each other. None of those prescriptions factor in when your body is best equipped to absorb them. ChronoRx does.
This is the highest-stakes age group for circadian medication errors. Blood pressure peaks in the morning, meaning antihypertensives taken at night reduce cardiovascular risk by up to 67% (HYGIA trial). Statins, metformin, and thyroid medications all have CYP450 enzyme dependencies that follow circadian rhythms. Timing them correctly isn't optional — it's clinical.
Your Biggest Circadian Challenges
Antihypertensives taken at the wrong time: The HYGIA trial showed a 67% reduction in cardiovascular events simply by shifting dosing from morning to bedtime.
Statins: CYP3A4 liver enzyme activity peaks at night. Statins taken at bedtime achieve significantly higher efficacy.
Metformin for Type 2 diabetes: Insulin sensitivity follows a circadian curve. Evening dosing aligns with natural metabolic rhythm.
Caregiver conflict: Optimal dosing times may not align with caregiver availability. The Caregiver Window Negotiator finds the best feasible slot.

🌅  Senior Citizens
Age Range: 60+ years
Your Medications Work Better at the Right Time. Let's Find That Time.
ChronoRx for Seniors — simple, safe, and clinically grounded medication timing
Why This Matters for Your Age Group
As we age, our circadian rhythms naturally shift earlier and weaken. This affects how every medication you take is absorbed, processed, and cleared. ChronoRx makes sense of it with a simple interface — no wearables, no complex setup — just clear guidance about when to take what.
Seniors take an average of 5+ medications daily. Circadian rhythm weakening in older adults affects drug metabolism significantly — CYP450 enzyme activity declines, liver clearance slows, and blood pressure rhythms flatten. Studies show that timing corrections alone can reduce adverse drug events by up to 30% in older patients. This is the highest-impact age group for ChronoRx.
Your Biggest Circadian Challenges
Polypharmacy complexity: 5+ medications from different specialists, each with different optimal windows. ChronoRx creates a single clear daily schedule.
Blood pressure variation: Morning BP surge is strongest in older adults. Taking antihypertensives at bedtime is particularly critical for this group (HYGIA evidence).
Missed doses: The Missed-Dose Rescue AI reasons biologically — not just by clock — about whether to take late or skip, reducing the risk of double-dosing.
Isolation and safety: The Contact Doctor trigger provides a safety net — automatically alerting a family member or GP when the schedule changes significantly.

Simple Daily Schedule Card

03
Caregiver / Family Coordinator

04
Sunrise Wake Window
Uses your location to calculate the exact sunrise time and your optimal light exposure window — supporting the natural circadian anchor that weakens with age.
05
Weekly Report for GP

06
Emergency Contact Doctor
